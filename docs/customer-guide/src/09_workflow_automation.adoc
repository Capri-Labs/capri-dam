== Workflow Automation & Approvals

=== 1. Feature Overview

**What it does:** A visual, drag-and-drop designer lets anyone build asset approval
and automation pipelines — no code required.

**The business value:** Reviewers get exactly the right assets at exactly the right
stage, automatically — no more chasing approvals over email or chat.

image::workflow-automation.png[Workflow Automation & Approvals, title="Placeholder — the visual workflow designer canvas (screenshot coming soon)"]

=== 2. How It Works

**The trigger:** A workflow starts when its configured trigger condition is met — most
commonly, an asset being uploaded into a scoped folder.

**What the system does:** The published workflow blueprint runs step by step: approval
tasks are assigned to the configured user or group, notifications are sent, and
condition steps branch the flow based on true/false logic.

**The output:** Task assignees see pending items in their Workflow Dashboard and
Inbox; once every step resolves, the workflow completes and any final actions
(e.g. auto-publish) run.

=== 3. Step-by-Step Instructions

. Open **Workflow Automation** from the sidebar and click **New Workflow**.
. Set the **trigger** (e.g. On Asset Upload) and its **scope** (all folders, or
  specific folders).
. Drag steps from the **node toolbox** onto the canvas: Approval, Parallel/Sequential
  Review, Send Email, In-App Alert, Slack Message, Delay, Webhook, or Condition.
. Connect the steps in order, assign reviewers to each Approval step.
. Click **Publish** — the designer validates your blueprint (e.g. every Approval step
  must have an assignee) before it goes live.

=== 4. Options & Configuration

* **Trigger scope** — apply a workflow to all folders, or to specific folders
  (with exclusions).
* **Condition branching** — TRUE/FALSE branching logic for conditional steps.
* **Workflow Dashboard** — a dedicated view of active and completed tasks, separate
  from the designer itself.

=== 5. Access & Permissions

**Roles required:** Building and publishing workflows requires Manager/Admin
permissions. Being assigned as a reviewer only requires being a valid user or member
of the assigned group — no special role is needed to complete an assigned task.

=== 6. System Limits & Technical Requirements

* The canvas supports a **fixed catalogue of step types** — there is currently no
  plugin/custom-node option for bespoke step types.
* **Condition branching is binary** (TRUE/FALSE) only — there is no multi-way
  switch/case branching yet.

=== 7. Troubleshooting & FAQs

**Why won't my workflow publish?**
The designer validates your blueprint before publish — a common cause is an Approval
step with no assignee. Check for a validation error message on the offending step.

**An asset isn't triggering my workflow. Why?**
Confirm the workflow is **published** (not just saved as a draft) and that the
uploaded asset actually falls within the trigger's configured folder scope.
