== Email Communication Engine

=== 1. Feature Overview

**What it does:** Built-in, brandable email notifications — for approvals, shares, and
workflow alerts — are designed visually, with no HTML required.

**The business value:** Your team stays in the loop automatically, with emails that
look and feel like your brand, not a generic system notification.

image::email-communication.png[Email Communication Engine, title="Placeholder — the visual email template designer (screenshot coming soon)"]

=== 2. How It Works

**The trigger:** A configured system event occurs — e.g. an asset is shared, or a
workflow task is assigned to you.

**What the system does:** The event is matched to its mapped email template. The
template is rendered with your organisation's branding (logo, colours, custom CSS)
and the relevant details of the event, then queued for delivery.

**The output:** The recipient receives the email, and a record of the send (with
delivery status) appears in the **Outbox** for administrators to review.

=== 3. Step-by-Step Instructions (for administrators)

. Open **Communication Engine** from the sidebar.
. Click **New Template**, choose a design from the gallery, or **Start from Blank**.
. Edit the template using the rich-text editor and the **Template Variables** picker.
. Preview the template in the sandboxed preview pane.
. Go to **Event Mapping** and map a system event to your new template.
. Configure **SMTP settings** and click **Test Connection** before committing, so mail
  delivery is verified up front.

=== 4. Options & Configuration

* **Branding** — logo, brand colours, and hand-editable custom CSS applied to every
  outgoing email.
* **AI Suggestions** — AI-assisted content suggestions inside the template editor.
* **SMTP test tools** — "Test Connection" and "Send SMTP Echo Check" verify delivery
  before it goes live.

=== 5. Access & Permissions

**Roles required:** Creating/editing templates, event mappings, and SMTP settings
requires Admin permissions. Receiving notification emails requires no special
permission — any user who is the subject of a mapped event will receive one.

=== 6. System Limits & Technical Requirements

* The **Send SMTP Echo Check** test-send requires a recipient address to be provided —
  there is no default "send to me" shortcut.
* AI Suggestions in the template editor is a content-assist surface — always review
  suggested copy before sending to customers or external partners.

=== 7. Troubleshooting & FAQs

**My users say they aren't receiving notification emails. What should I check first?**
Ask an admin to open **SMTP Settings** and run **Test Connection** — a broken SMTP
credential is the most common cause, and this test surfaces the exact error.

**Can I see whether a specific email was actually sent?**
Yes — check the **Outbox** tab, which is a paginated delivery log of every email the
system has sent, along with its status.
